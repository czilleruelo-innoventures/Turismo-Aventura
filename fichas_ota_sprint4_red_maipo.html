"A 60 km de Santiago, los Andes te esperan — sin vuelo, sin traslado largo, sin logística compleja."
🇺🇸 English
Escape the city and dive into one of the most dramatic Andean landscapes in South America — just 60 km from downtown Santiago. This full-day small-group adventure takes you deep into Cajón del Maipo, a mountain canyon carved by glacial rivers at the foot of a 5,800m volcano.
Your journey includes a guided hike along the turquoise El Yeso reservoir at 2,500m altitude, a traditional Chilean lunch at a local family restaurant, and optional white-water rafting on the Maipo River. Expert bilingual guides share stories of the local arriero culture, Andean ecology, and regional history throughout the day.
Small groups (max. 12), certified guides, full safety equipment, and door-to-door pickup from your Santiago hotel included. No experience required.

El recorrido incluye una caminata guiada por CasaBosque Exp. junto al embalse El Yeso a 2.500 m de altitud, almuerzo tradicional en Hostería Millahue, y rafting opcional con Isoterma Turismo en el río Maipo (clase 3-4). Guías bilingües certificados, grupos máximo 12 personas, traslado desde tu hotel en Santiago.

"Subirse a un caballo criollo con un arriero de 70 años que conoce cada piedra de la Cordillera — eso no se consigue en ninguna OTA del mundo."
🇺🇸 English
In the Andes, the arrieros — Chile's legendary mountain horsemen — have navigated these mountain passes for centuries, carrying cattle, cargo, and stories across the high Cordillera. This 2-day immersion connects you directly with this living tradition, guided by local arriero families who still live and work these trails today.
Day 1: Depart Santiago, meet your arriero guide, mount a criollo horse and ride through high Andean valleys with views of glaciers and extinct volcanoes. Night falls under some of South America's darkest skies — an astronomer joins for a guided stargazing session with professional telescope.
Day 2: Sunrise over the Andes, traditional breakfast, final ride and return to Santiago. Everything included: accommodation, all meals, bilingual guide, astronomer, horses, and equipment. Small group — max. 8 people.

🇺🇸 English
This is not a hiking tour. This is five days living the Andes the way those who truly know them live — walking light while mules carry the load, sleeping in a mountain refuge at altitude, bathing in natural thermal springs, and sharing an asado under the stars with Andean arrieros.
Led by Jaime Acevedo (Ecocaminante), a UIMLA-certified mountain guide and geologist-storyteller, the expedition takes you deep into the Cajón del Maipo — past the Baños Azules, through glacial valleys, up to the Refugio Tupungato. Every kilometer is narrated: the geology, the culture, the silence.
All-inclusive: transport from Santiago, certified guide and team, mule logistics, refuge accommodation, full meals (4 breakfasts, 4 trail rations, 4 dinners), thermal springs, summit asado, and a final night at Rancho El Añil with spa. Max 8–10 participants. Intermediate to advanced fitness required.
🇨🇱 Español
No es un trekking turístico. Son cinco días viviendo los Andes como quienes los conocen de verdad: caminando sin peso (las mulas van primero), durmiendo en un refugio de altura, bañándose en termas rústicas y compartiendo un asado bajo las estrellas con arrieros reales.
Guiada por Jaime Acevedo (Ecocaminante), guía UIMLA y geólogo-narrador, la expedición recorre el Cajón del Maipo en profundidad: Baños Azules, valles glaciares, el Refugio Tupungato. Cada kilómetro tiene historia, geología y cultura que Jaime convierte en relato.
Todo incluido: traslado desde Santiago, guías certificados, logística de mulas, alojamiento en refugio y carpa, alimentación completa (4 desayunos, 4 raciones de marcha, 4 cenas), termas, asado de cumbre y última noche en Rancho El Añil con spa. Máx. 8–10 personas. Nivel físico intermedio–avanzado.
